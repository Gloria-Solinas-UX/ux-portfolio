Self Reflection

November, 23 2025
My Journey

This portfolio project showcases my skill set and reflects my professional identity. Drawing on my skills audit, it highlights my technical strengths in Web development, Accessibility, and UI/UX Design, as well as key non-technical skills such as problem-solving, creativity, and critical thinking, demonstrated through two strong case studies.

Creative Direction

My moodboard takes inspiration from clean, minimal designs that use simple layouts and accessible typography to keep the focus on content rather than decoration. This clarity-first approach reflects my commitment to usability and accessibility, aligning with my practice of creating functional, user-centred experiences where design enhances the message instead of competing with it.

Anatomy of an Idea

These initial sketches helped me clarify layout priorities for the homepage and case study pages. By roughly mapping content hierarchy, CTAs, and navigation patterns, I could quickly explore structure without visual distractions. The process highlighted what should remain static versus scrollable, shaping clearer, more intentional design decisions.

Building With Intention

After evaluating several page-builder frameworks, I chose Bootstrap as the best option to truly demonstrate my skills. Tools like Framer or WordPress rely heavily on plug-ins and minimal coding, which limits opportunities to showcase technical expertise and can become costly long term (Popescu, 2025). Bootstrap, on the other hand, enables me to build a responsive, scalable, and easy to maintain website (Tompalmerwebsites.co.uk, 2025) while actively applying my knowledge of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. By using semantic, accessible code throughout, the website itself becomes a tangible, hands-on proof of what I can technically do.

“I believe successful UX/UI design is rooted in understanding how digital products are made. Coding isn’t optional, it informs my decisions, improves collaboration, and grounds my design in reality.”
