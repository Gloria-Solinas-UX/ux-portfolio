Portfolio Reflection

November, 23 2025
My Journey

This portfolio project showcases my skill set and reflects my professional identity. Drawing on my skills audit, it highlights my technical strengths in Web development, Accessibility, and UI/UX Design, as well as key non-technical skills such as problem-solving, creativity, and critical thinking.

Creative Direction

My moodboard takes inspiration from clean, minimal designs that use simple layouts and accessible typography to keep the focus on content rather than decoration. This clarity-first approach reflects my commitment to usability and accessibility, aligning with my practice of creating functional, user-centred experiences where design enhances the message instead of competing with it.

Anatomy of an Idea

These initial sketches helped me clarify layout priorities for the homepage and case study pages. By roughly mapping content hierarchy, CTAs, and navigation patterns, I could quickly explore structure without visual distractions. The process highlighted what should remain static versus scrollable, shaping clearer, more intentional design decisions.

Building With Intention

After reviewing different page-builder frameworks, including WordPress, I chose Bootstrap to clearly demonstrate my technical skills rather than rely on plug-in-heavy tools with limited flexibility. Bootstrap allows me to build a responsive, scalable, and maintainable website while actively applying semantic HTML, CSS, and JavaScript (Tompalmerwebsites.co.uk, 2025), making the site itself acting as tangible proof of my technical ability. I then decided to host my portfolio website on GitHub Pages because it provides a simple, free, and reliable solution for deploying a static website, reinforcing my preference for transparent, code-driven workflows over closed or costly platforms (Howell, 2017).

“I believe successful UX/UI design is rooted in understanding how digital products are made. Coding isn’t optional, it informs my decisions and grounds my design in reality.”
Visual DNA: Colours, Typography & Style

My initial moodboard focused on a modern and minimal aesthetic but lacked clear structure. Through iteration, I purposefully and strategically combined colours to ensure high contrast across all deliverables and paired them with the Inter system font to further enhance accessibility and usability, which are at the core of my design approach and philosophy.

First Ideas taking Shape

These early designs highlighted visual imbalance and inconsistency. The hierarchy and spacing didn’t align with the clean, minimal aesthetic I had in mind. This stage helped me recognise the need for stronger visual harmony and clearer structure, especially around the case study cards, which felt visually noisy with too many elements competing for attention.

Feedback as a Design tool

Peer and industry feedback highlighted bsome visual issues, particularly around visual hierarchy, perceived interactivity, and engagement. Issues such unreadable text on images, visually noisy cards, and a static homepage highlighted how design intent does not always translate to user perception. Through iteration, I learned the importance of testing visual clarity, hierarchy, and affordances early on, rather than relying solely on my own judgement and assumption. Introducing clearer card structures, hover interactions, and improved navigation reinforced how small interaction cues can significantly enhance usability. This process strengthened my understanding of feedback as a critical design tool for creating more engaging, user-centred solutions.

Final outcomes: Consistency in action

In response to feedback, I aligned visual language across digital and printed outputs, refining typography, layout, and aesthetics. The final website, LinkedIn profile page, and business card fully showcase the application of a clear, consistent brand identity. This visual consistency builds user trust and reinforces strong brand recognition (Frontify.com, 2025).
